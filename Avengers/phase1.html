The Incredible Hulk

After being exposed to gamma radiation that causes him to transform into the monstrous Hulk, scientist Bruce Banner goes on the run and isolates himself from his love, Betty Ross. Hunted by the military, Banner seeks to cure himself and prevent his condition from being weaponized

In January 2006,[44] Marvel reclaimed the film rights for the Hulk character from Universal Pictures after Universal failed to meet a deadline to develop a sequel to director Ang Lee's 2003 film Hulk.[45] Universal retained distribution rights for future Hulk films.[45] Instead of moving forward with a sequel, Marvel hired Louis Leterrier to direct The Incredible Hulk, a reboot.[19] Leterrier initially turned down the job out of respect for Lee, but later reconsidered and signed on.[19] The script was written by Zak Penn, who drafted a treatment for the 2003 film.

The film takes place simultaneously with the events of Iron Man 2 and Thor,[52] the former of which is set six months after the events of Iron Man.[53] Downey briefly reprised his role from Iron Man as Tony Stark in a cameo appearance at the end of the film. Downey said that the filmmakers "were just cross-pollinating our superheroes. It happens to be a scene where I basically approach [actor William Hurt's character General Ross], and we may be considering going into some sort of limited partnership together.
